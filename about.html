Our Philosophy

WTX Mountain Fitness was created with the intention of blending the pursuit of fitness and health with a desire to get reacquainted with nature and all the fun and rewarding activities it provides. Workouts don't have to be tedious, nor do they have to be done in the gym. Ultimately, we believe that living intentionally, expanding our capabilities, and pursuing self-mastery lead to us being healthier, happier, and more fulfilled.

While modern society has eased many burdens of daily life, it has also outsourced core competencies critical for independent survival. We feel empowered when we reclaim these fundamental skills and practice them regularly. It enables us to tackle challenges with calm competence, whether in civilization or the wilderness.

We hope you will join us on the journey toward becoming equipped for whatever life brings- or what you choose to conquer.

Physical Fitness

Regular exercise is crucial for optimal health, yet many people struggle to fit it into their routine or see it as a chore. We aim to build functional fitness and prepare your body to handle whatever life throws at you - whether it's hauling heavy loads on a backcountry hike or running for the bus. We'll cover proper form, gear recommendations, and how to make fitness a lifelong habit. There's no one-size-fits-all approach, so we'll explore customized programs based on your unique goals and abilities. Physical vitality promotes confidence, focus, and an empowered mindset to take on challenges.

Survival & Wilderness Skills

Modern amenities have enabled many of us to spend less time in nature. However, reconnecting with our primal roots in the outdoors provides immense benefits. Beyond survival basics like starting fires and finding your way home, we'll teach you how to thrive comfortably outdoors through trip planning, gear selection, and Leave No Trace ethics. Cultivating these abilities allows you to experience nature more intimately, safely, and with confidence. It builds awareness, grit, adaptability, and appreciation for the natural world.

Preparedness

Today's world is increasingly vulnerable to disasters, both natural and man-made. The antidote to fear and uncertainty is knowledge, and we'd like to share it. We can incorporate elements of at-home and on-the-go "prepping", basic first aid, and situational awareness techniques into other training programs, or create a stand-alone program if that's your focus. Self-reliance reduces panic during catastrophes and provides comfort that you can handle whatever comes your way.

Take the First Step
